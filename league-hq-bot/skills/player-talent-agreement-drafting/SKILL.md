## Overview

This skill drafts the agreements a sports league/IP business signs with the people side of the business: players/athletes competing in the league, and talent (commentators, ambassadors, influencers, guest appearances) at events. It produces a first-draft document, not final legal paperwork — always close with the disclaimer below.

**Disclaimer to always include:** This is a working draft to speed up negotiation, not legal advice. A lawyer should review before either party signs, especially clauses on image rights, exclusivity, and termination.

## Ask before drafting

1. **Which type of agreement** — player/athlete participation agreement, appearance agreement (single event or short-term), or an image/likeness (NIL-style) rights agreement?
2. **Term** — single event, single season, multi-season?
3. **Compensation structure** — flat fee, per-event fee, performance/prize-based, revenue share, or a mix?
4. **Exclusivity** — does the league need the player/talent to be exclusive to it during the term (can't play for/appear at competing leagues or events)?
5. Any prior agreement of the same type in the project to match tone/terms for consistency across the roster.

## Key clauses to include

### Player/athlete participation agreements
- Parties, term, and event/season schedule the player commits to
- Compensation (fee structure, payment schedule, bonuses tied to performance or attendance)
- Exclusivity / non-compete (can they play in rival leagues during the term?)
- Image & likeness rights (league's right to use the player's name/image for marketing — specify scope: social, broadcast, print, geographic reach)
- Conduct clause (behavior standards, doping/eligibility rules if relevant to the sport)
- Injury & insurance (who covers medical costs, what happens to compensation if injured mid-season)
- Equipment/uniform obligations
- Termination (for cause, for injury, for underperformance) and notice periods
- Renewal/option terms for following seasons

### Appearance / talent agreements (commentators, ambassadors, guest appearances)
- Scope of appearance (specific event(s), dates, duration, required activities — e.g. commentary, meet-and-greet, social posts)
- Fee and payment timing (often paid faster than season-long deals — e.g. net-15 after appearance)
- Travel & accommodation responsibility
- Image rights for promotional use of the appearance (before/after the event, on which channels)
- Exclusivity (are they barred from appearing for a competing league/event around the same date?)
- Cancellation terms (what happens if either side cancels, weather/force majeure)

### Image/likeness (NIL-style) rights agreements
- Precise scope of rights granted (which images/footage, which channels, geographic reach, duration of usage rights — note usage rights can outlive the playing relationship)
- Whether rights are exclusive or non-exclusive to the league
- Compensation (flat fee vs. royalty/revenue share tied to merchandise or media using their likeness)
- Approval rights (does the player get to approve specific uses, e.g. no use in categories they conflict with — relevant if they have a personal sponsor in a competing category)
- Termination and what happens to existing published content using their likeness after termination

## Output format

Default to a **docx** draft agreement with numbered clauses and defined terms (capitalize and define terms like "the League," "the Player," "the Term" on first use). If the user wants a quick internal summary of terms before drafting full legal language, offer a short bullet list of proposed terms first for their sign-off, then draft the full document.
